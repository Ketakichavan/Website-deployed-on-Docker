Botanical Boosters

Timeless Home Remedies for Your Garden

Natural Fertilizers

Banana Peels: Bury banana peels in soil to enrich it with potassium and phosphorus. Use every 2 weeks.
Eggshells: Crushed eggshells add calcium to the soil, helping plant growth. Apply once a month.
Compost Tea: Soak compost in water for a nutrient-rich liquid fertilizer. Use every 2 weeks.
Coffee Grounds: Sprinkle used coffee grounds around plants to improve nitrogen levels. Apply once a month.

Organic Pesticides

Neem Oil/Water Spray: Mix neem oil with water and spray on plants to keep pests away naturally. Use every 10 days.
Garlic Spray: Blend garlic and water, strain, and spray to repel harmful insects. Apply once a week.
Chili Pepper Spray: Mix crushed chili peppers with water to deter pests like aphids and caterpillars. Use every 2 weeks.
Soap Water: Mix mild liquid soap with water to eliminate soft-bodied insects like aphids and whiteflies. Apply once a week.

Companion Planting

Marigolds: Plant marigolds around your garden to repel nematodes and pests naturally. Plant once per season.
Basil: Growing basil near tomatoes enhances their growth and deters mosquitoes and flies. Replant every 3 months.
Lavender: Lavender deters moths, fleas, and mosquitoes while adding a pleasant fragrance to your garden. Prune twice a year.
