How to Grow a Healthy Periwinkle Plant

Temperature

20°C to 30°C

Water

1-2 times/week

Planting Steps

  1. Choose a well-draining garden spot or container.
  2. Loosen soil and mix in compost.
  3. Plant periwinkle seedlings or cuttings 6–12 inches apart.
  4. Water thoroughly after planting.

1. Choose the Right Periwinkle Type

  • Common types: Vinca major (larger leaves), Vinca minor (ground cover).
  • Choose based on desired size and space coverage.
  • Type: Well-draining, sandy or loamy soil.
  • pH Level: Slightly acidic to neutral (6.0–7.0).
  • Preparation: Add compost or organic matter to enrich the soil.
  • Water regularly until the plant is established.
  • Afterward, it becomes drought-tolerant with minimal watering.
  • Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.
  • Organic Options: Compost, worm castings, seaweed extract.
  • Chemical Fertilizers: Balanced NPK (10-10-10) during growing season.
  • Fertilize monthly in spring and summer.
  • Partial shade to full sun areas are ideal.
  • Can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
  • Perfect for borders, slopes, and containers.

Final Tips

Trim back excessive growth to keep it tidy. Great as ground cover for erosion control. Periwinkle is low maintenance and grows fast in favorable conditions.

Periwinkle Plant Care Tips

  • Pruning: Light pruning helps control spread and encourages dense growth.
  • Pest Control: Rarely affected by pests; occasional aphids or spider mites may appear.
  • Disease Prevention: Ensure good air circulation to prevent fungal issues like leaf spot.
