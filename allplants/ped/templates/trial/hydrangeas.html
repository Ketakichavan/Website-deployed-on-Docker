Planting Steps

  1. Dig a hole twice as wide as the root ball and just as deep.
  2. Amend the soil with compost or organic matter.
  3. Place the hydrangea and cover the roots with soil.
  4. Water thoroughly and add a layer of mulch.

1. Choose the Right Hydrangea Variety

  • Popular types: Bigleaf, Panicle, Oakleaf, Smooth Hydrangeas.
  • Choose based on sunlight, soil, and size preferences.
  • Type: Moist, well-draining soil enriched with organic matter.
  • pH Level: 5.5 to 6.5 (affects flower color in some varieties).
  • Preparation: Mix compost and peat moss before planting.
  • Hydrangeas need regular watering, especially in the first 2 years.
  • Keep the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged.
  • Water early in the day to reduce evaporation.
  • Organic Options: Compost, fish emulsion, slow-release natural fertilizer.
  • Chemical Fertilizers: Balanced 10-10-10 or bloom-boosting formulas.
  • Apply in spring and once more in mid-summer if needed.
  • Partial shade with morning sun and afternoon shade is ideal.
  • Ensure good air circulation to prevent fungal diseases.
  • Protect from strong winds and harsh sun.
