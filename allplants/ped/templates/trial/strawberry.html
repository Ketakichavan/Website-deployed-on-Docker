How to Grow a Healthy Strawberry Plant

Temperature

5°C to 20°C

Water

2–3 times/week

Planting Steps

  1. Choose a sunny location with well-draining soil.
  2. Dig small holes and space plants 12–18 inches apart.
  3. Place roots straight down and crown at soil level.
  4. Water thoroughly after planting.

1. Choose the Right Strawberry Variety

  • June-bearing, Everbearing, and Day-neutral are main types.
  • Choose based on climate and harvest preferences.
  • Type: Loamy, well-draining soil enriched with compost.
  • pH Level: Slightly acidic (5.5–6.8).
  • Preparation: Mix in aged manure and organic matter.
  • Keep soil consistently moist, especially during fruiting.
  • Water early in the day to allow foliage to dry.
  • Use drip irrigation or water at the base to avoid leaf diseases.
  • Organic Options: Compost, fish emulsion, aged manure.
  • Chemical Fertilizers: Balanced fertilizer like 10-10-10 applied before flowering.
  • Fertilize lightly during growing season, avoid overfeeding.
  • Full sun (at least 6–8 hours daily).
  • Plant in raised beds or containers for better drainage.
  • Rotate planting spot yearly to prevent soil-borne diseases.

Final Tips

Mulch with straw to retain moisture and keep fruits clean. Pinch off runners for larger berries. Protect plants from birds with netting.

Strawberry Plant Care Tips

  • Pruning: Remove yellow leaves and old runners to focus energy on fruiting.
  • Pest Control: Watch for slugs, aphids, and spider mites; use organic deterrents.
  • Disease Prevention: Avoid overhead watering; space plants well for air circulation.
